Seasonal Care

Preparing your vehicle for seasonal weather changes and conditions.

Before performing seasonal maintenance:

  • Complete all basic maintenance tasks first
  • Identify which season you're preparing for
  • Consider your local climate and typical weather conditions
  • Gather appropriate tools and supplies

Vehicles require specific preparations for different seasons to ensure optimal performance and safety in changing weather conditions. Seasonal maintenance helps prevent weather-related failures and extends vehicle longevity.

Winter Preparation
  1. Check battery condition and charging system

    Cold weather reduces battery capacity, often by 50% in extreme cold. Battery failures are most common during winter months.

    1. Have battery professionally tested to verify cranking capacity
    2. Clean battery terminals and connections
    3. Check charging system output (alternator)
    4. Consider replacing batteries more than three years old before winter begins
  2. Inspect cooling system and antifreeze protection

    Proper antifreeze protection prevents cooling system damage in freezing temperatures.

    1. Check coolant level and condition
    2. Test freeze protection with an antifreeze tester
    3. Ensure protection to at least -30째F (-34째C) in cold climates
    4. Inspect for leaks in hoses, radiator, and water pump
    5. Consider a cooling system flush if it's been more than two years
  3. Prepare tires for winter conditions

    Proper tires are critical for winter traction and safety.

    1. Consider installing dedicated winter tires in snowy regions
    2. Verify tire pressure regularly (pressure drops in cold weather)
    3. Check tread depth - minimum 3/32" for winter driving
    4. Consider carrying tire chains where legally required
  4. Prepare visibility systems

    Winter conditions often reduce visibility, making proper vehicle lighting critical.

    1. Replace worn wiper blades with winter-specific blades if available
    2. Fill washer reservoir with winter-grade fluid (freeze-resistant)
    3. Clean all exterior lights and check for proper operation
    4. Consider applying water-repellent treatment to windshield
  5. Prepare emergency winter kit

    Winter breakdowns can be dangerous in cold weather.

    1. Include blanket, gloves, hat, and warm clothing
    2. Pack flashlight with fresh batteries
    3. Include ice scraper, snow brush, and small shovel
    4. Pack jumper cables or portable jump starter
    5. Include sand, cat litter, or traction mats
    6. Pack high-energy snacks and water
    7. Consider road flares or emergency reflectors
Summer Preparation
  1. Inspect cooling system for hot weather

    Summer heat increases stress on the cooling system, which must prevent engine overheating.

    1. Check coolant level and condition
    2. Inspect hoses for bulges, cracks, or leaks
    3. Test radiator cap for proper pressure holding
    4. Check cooling fan operation (electric or belt-driven)
    5. Consider a cooling system flush if it's been more than two years
  2. Check air conditioning performance

    A properly functioning air conditioning system is essential for summer comfort and safety.

    1. Test A/C output temperature (should be 40-45째F/4-7째C at vents)
    2. Check for unusual noises during operation
    3. Replace cabin air filter if clogged
    4. Have system professionally serviced if cooling is inadequate
  3. Prepare tires for summer conditions

    Hot pavement increases risk of tire failure and affects handling.

    1. Check tire pressure when cold (pressure increases in hot weather)
    2. Inspect for abnormal wear patterns
    3. Rotate tires if it's been more than 6,000 miles
    4. Switch from winter to all-season or summer tires if applicable
  4. Check underbody and suspension components

    Winter road salt and chemicals can cause corrosion and damage.

    1. Inspect for rust and corrosion, particularly on brake lines
    2. Check for damaged suspension components
    3. Lubricate hinges, locks, and linkages as needed
    4. Consider professional underbody wash to remove salt residue

Following these seasonal maintenance procedures helps ensure your vehicle operates safely and reliably in changing weather conditions, preventing breakdowns and extending vehicle life.

In regions with distinct seasons, perform winter preparation in late fall (October/November) and summer preparation in late spring (April/May) to ensure your vehicle is ready before seasonal weather changes occur.
