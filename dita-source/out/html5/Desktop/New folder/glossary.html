Automotive Glossary

Definitions of common automotive terms and abbreviations.

Engine Terms

Term Definition
Bore The diameter of each cylinder in an engine
Stroke The distance the piston travels up and down within the cylinder
Displacement The total volume of air/fuel mixture an engine can draw in during one complete engine cycle
Horsepower (HP) A unit of power equal to 550 foot-pounds per second or 746 watts
Torque A twisting force that tends to cause rotation
Compression ratio The ratio of the volume of the cylinder with the piston at bottom dead center to the volume with the piston at top dead center
Turbocharger A device that increases an engine's power by forcing compressed air into the combustion chamber, driven by exhaust gases
Supercharger A device that increases an engine's power by forcing compressed air into the combustion chamber, driven mechanically by the engine

Drivetrain Terms

Term Definition
Transmission A mechanical system that transfers power from the engine to the wheels, providing different gear ratios
Clutch A mechanism for connecting and disconnecting the engine from the transmission
Differential A gear system that allows wheels to rotate at different speeds while cornering
FWD Front-Wheel Drive: power delivered to the front wheels
RWD Rear-Wheel Drive: power delivered to the rear wheels
AWD All-Wheel Drive: power delivered to all four wheels, typically with varying distribution
4WD Four-Wheel Drive: power can be delivered to all four wheels, typically with selectable engagement
CVT Continuously Variable Transmission: provides seamless ratio changes without distinct gear shifts

Safety Terms

Term Definition
ABS Anti-lock Braking System: prevents wheel lockup during braking
ESC/ESP Electronic Stability Control/Program: helps maintain directional control during emergency maneuvers
TCS Traction Control System: prevents wheel spin during acceleration
ADAS Advanced Driver Assistance Systems: technologies that assist the driver
ACC Adaptive Cruise Control: maintains set speed and following distance
FCW Forward Collision Warning: alerts driver to potential frontal collisions
AEB Automatic Emergency Braking: applies brakes autonomously to prevent or mitigate collisions
BLIS Blind Spot Information System: monitors areas not visible in mirrors

Electric Vehicle Terms

Term Definition
BEV Battery Electric Vehicle: powered solely by electricity
HEV Hybrid Electric Vehicle: combines internal combustion engine with electric propulsion
PHEV Plug-in Hybrid Electric Vehicle: hybrid with external charging capability
kWh Kilowatt-hour: unit of energy storage capacity in batteries
Regenerative braking System that recovers energy during deceleration
Level 1/2/3 charging Classifications of EV charging speeds (120V, 240V, and high-voltage DC)
